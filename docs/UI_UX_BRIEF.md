# Salon POS UI/UX Brief

## Design System

The interface should feel minimal, premium, calm, and operational. It should support fast daily use by salon owners and reception staff without requiring technical knowledge.

Design principles:

- Keep screens focused on the next action.
- Use clear hierarchy and readable spacing.
- Prefer search-first workflows for billing, customers, services, and products.
- Use salon-oriented wording throughout.
- Keep actions obvious with large touch-friendly buttons.
- Keep dashboard summaries visual but not noisy.

## Color Palette

- Background: warm white or very light neutral.
- Text: deep neutral/navy for strong readability.
- Primary: deep navy for stable business actions.
- Accent: teal for active states and positive highlights.
- Success: green for completed actions and healthy status.
- Warning: amber for low stock or attention states.
- Danger: red for delete, inactive, and blocked actions.

Avoid dominant dark screens, heavy gradients, and overly saturated palettes.

## Typography

- Use clean sans-serif typography.
- Use large readable text for operational actions.
- Reserve large headings for dashboard/page titles.
- Keep form labels short.
- Avoid dense paragraphs inside app screens.

## Component Guidelines

- Buttons: large enough for touch use, clear command labels, icons where helpful.
- Inputs: clear labels, placeholders only as secondary guidance.
- Cards: use for dashboard metrics and repeated entities.
- Dialogs: use for create/edit flows when the task is short.
- Tables/lists: clean, scannable, and responsive.
- Badges: use for active/inactive, role, customer category, and low stock.
- Toasts/messages: short, actionable, and plain.

## Dashboard Layout

- Persistent sidebar on desktop.
- Compact top header with page title and user context.
- Summary cards at top.
- Operational lists and analytics below.
- Staff dashboards emphasize today, week, month, recent services, and commission.
- Admin dashboard emphasizes revenue, customers, bills, low stock, and staff performance.

## Sidebar Structure

Recommended admin sidebar:

- Dashboard
- Billing
- Services
- Inventory
- Staff
- Customers
- Reports
- Performance
- Reminders
- Settings
- Logout

Cashier sidebar should focus on billing, customers, services, inventory, reminders, and logout. Barber, Stylist, and Beautician sidebars should focus on personal dashboard/performance and logout.

## Mobile Responsiveness

- Sidebar should collapse or stack.
- Buttons must remain finger-friendly.
- Cards should wrap into one column.
- Billing sections should stack cleanly.
- Important totals and payment actions should stay visible near confirmation.
- Forms should avoid horizontal overflow.

## User Experience Goals

- A cashier can complete a normal bill quickly.
- A staff member can understand their performance in seconds.
- An admin can identify top staff, best services, revenue, low stock, and repeat customers quickly.
- Error messages should explain what to fix.
- The interface should work comfortably on a tablet at reception.

## Accessibility Guidelines

- Maintain strong color contrast.
- Do not rely on color alone for status.
- Use visible focus states.
- Use semantic buttons and form controls.
- Keep text legible at standard browser zoom.
- Ensure modals have obvious close/cancel options.
- Avoid hover-only critical actions.

## Design Principles

- Minimal: show what staff need now.
- Elegant: polished spacing, quiet color, and consistent components.
- Premium: professional dashboards and receipts.
- Salon-oriented: services, staff, customers, products, commissions, and reminders.
- Fast workflow: fewer clicks, clear defaults, and strong validation.
